## What Is a Live Stream Operator?

A Live Stream Operator is a **technical and organizational role** behind the scenes of live streamers. The operator ensures broadcasts run smoothly -- managing equipment, software, and audience interaction -- so the streamer can focus on content.

**Key analogy:** Think of it as being a sound engineer at a radio station -- you manage the tech, but you are never on air.

### Core Responsibilities

- Setting up and controlling streaming equipment (PC, audio)
- Working with streaming software (OBS, Streamlabs) to optimize image and sound quality
- Moderating live chats: responding to messages, blocking rule violators, removing spam/ads/negative comments
- Planning and organizing stream sessions
- Supporting streamers with content plan creation and adjustments
- Proposing creative ideas (encouraged by the company)

### What This Role Is NOT

- The operator **NEVER appears on camera**
- No content creation required
- No prior streaming experience needed
- This is a **behind-the-scenes** position
