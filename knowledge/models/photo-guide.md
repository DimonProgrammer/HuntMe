# Candidate Photo Approval Procedure

> Источник: Training Examples EN/START_HERE_Documentation/Models/03_Photo_Approval_Guide_Models.html

*Internal Guide for Recruitment Team*

---

## 1. Number and Recency of Photos

Request **2-3 recent photos** taken recently. The photos must reflect the candidate's **current appearance**.

## 2. Full-Length Photo

A **full-body photo is mandatory** in order to properly assess overall proportions and visual format.

## 3. No Editing or Filters

Photos must be:
- without filters
- without retouching
- without graphic editing
- without distortion of proportions

It is important to see the candidate's **real appearance**.

## 4. Photos from Messengers

Photos sent through messengers are **not recommended**, as it is impossible to verify when they were taken and whether they are up to date.

## 5. Photos from Social Media

Photos from social media are acceptable only if:
- they were posted recently (no more than 3 months ago);
- they reflect the candidate's current appearance.

---

## Evaluation Criteria

When reviewing photos, the following aspects are considered:
- overall visual format (consistency with stated physical parameters)
- on-screen presentation
- grooming and neatness
- general presentation

It is important to understand that streaming is a visual digital product.

In live formats, first impressions are formed primarily through visual presentation.

This is not about discrimination or subjective preference. It is about **market demand and commercial effectiveness**.

In the live streaming industry, audiences respond first and foremost to:
- on-screen presence
- grooming and self-presentation
- the ability to create a pleasant and aesthetically appealing image

Even with strong support, promotion, and team involvement, **the streamer's on-screen presentation plays a significant role in audience engagement and commercial performance**.

Therefore, initial visual screening is not about strictness -- it is a tool for assessing the candidate's commercial potential within a specific market.

## Visual Format Examples

To simplify the approval process, the original document provides visual examples. The purpose of these examples is to clearly demonstrate the difference between:
- a candidate who matches the format
- a candidate who does not match the format

**This helps to:**
- minimize subjective interpretation
- reduce the number of disputed cases
- standardize primary selection criteria
- lower rejection rates at the interview stage

### Does NOT match the platform's visual format

Candidates who do not align with the platform's on-screen visual presentation standards. Characteristics:
- physical parameters outside the formula threshold (height - weight < 100)
- poor grooming or self-presentation
- visual format inconsistent with market demand

### Matches the platform's visual format

Candidates who align with the platform's visual and on-screen format. Characteristics:
- physical parameters within the formula threshold (height - weight >= 100)
- good grooming and self-presentation
- visual format consistent with market demand
- pleasant on-screen presence

## Key Takeaway

**It is important to understand:** This is not a personal evaluation of an individual or their worth. It is strictly about compliance with the professional requirements of the live-content market.
