### **Chapter 1 — The Warm-Up System That Builds Trust**

### **Objective:**

Train your Business Managers and Pixels to look safe before sending aggressive traffic.

### **The Neutral Ad Buffer Method:**

- For every ***1 agressive*** ad, run 4–5 neutral ads.
- Each neutral ad runs at ***$1–$2/day.***
- Choose low-risk verticals like ***gadgets, beauty, pets, lifestyle, or accessories.***
- Keep creatives simple: static image + short headline + CTA.

### **Duplication Strategy**

- Duplicate WH ads frequently to inflate your approval ratio.
- Small edits (different image or CTA) make the system think you’re testing variations.
- This raises your approval percentage and builds a healthy ad history.

### **Activity Rotation**

- Never repeat the same audience, GEO, or interest targeting on your neutral ads.
- Rotate every 48 hours.
- This creates the illusion of genuine testing activity — a key “normal” advertiser signal.

### **Chapter 2: Launching Aggressive Campaigns**

Now that your BM is warmed up and trusted, you can begin testing and launching aggressive creatives.

### **Step 1: Start With Minimal Spend**

When launching aggressive creatives, **start small.**

Your goal isn’t to scale yet — it’s to confirm that the ads actually spend without triggering review issues.

- Begin with a ***$2–$4 daily budget per ad*** to test approval and delivery.
- Once the ad starts delivering normally (even a few impressions), you know it has cleared the automated filters.
- From there, you can duplicate and increase budget safely in later stages.

If the creative doesn’t spend or gets rejected immediately, **don’t edit or appeal.**

Instead, duplicate it and make small changes — new text layer, cropped image, or slightly different video intro. The system treats duplicates as “fresh” uploads and often approves them even when the original failed.

### **Step 2 (Optional): Android or Emulator Logins**

For advanced operators, logging into ad profiles through **Android devices or mobile emulators** can help improve approval rates and reduce random verification prompts.

This isn’t mandatory — it’s simply an **extra safety layer** for high-risk setups or aged profiles you manage manually.

If you’re not managing multiple environments, you can skip this entirely.

Focus on creative testing first — that’s where the real approval logic lies.

### **Step 3: Timing Windows**

The best times to submit new ads for review are **late night (11 PM–2 AM)** or **early morning (5–7 AM)** in your account’s local timezone.

There’s less human moderation during these windows, so creatives often slip through automated review faster.

### **Step 4: Layered Upload Technique**

If an ad gets rejected:

- Upload the ***background*** only first.
- Then add ***text.***
- Then add ***sound*** or ***voiceover.***

If it still fails, overlay your video on a **clean background clip** and reduce brightness slightly, this often passes automated filters.

### **Step 5: Objective Switching**

- Launch campaigns first using the ***Video Views*** objective.These get approved much faster.
- Once approved and spending, duplicate the ***campaign*** and switch to ***Website Traffic*** or ***Conversions.***

This way, you isolate whether a rejection comes from your ad creative or your landing page.

### **Chapter 3: Scaling Without Detection**

Scaling is where most advertisers lose their accounts.

Meta doesn’t punish spending — it punishes unnatural behavior.

The key is to **scale naturally,** keeping your spend growth smooth and believable.

### **Smart Budget Framework**

- Start conversion campaigns at ***$49/day.***
- After 48 hours of stable performance, ***scale to $99/day.***
- Beyond ***$100/day,*** it’s recommended to ***split across multiple accounts.***However, some advertisers prefer to ***keep scaling the same ad account*** as long as it stays stable and keeps spending normally — this can also work if the account’s health remains strong.

The rule is simple:

> If it’s alive and stable, let it run.
> 

But always have backup ad accounts ready in case it flags suddenly.

### **Campaign Separation**

Keep **CBO** and **BID** campaigns on separate ad accounts.

Never mix both strategies inside the same account — it confuses the delivery signals and increases the risk of detection.

### **Smart Scaling Rules**

- Increase budgets by ***no more than 20–30% per day.***
- Avoid editing live campaigns — ***duplicate instead.***
- Watch for ***sudden CPM spikes*** or major drops in ***CTR*** — these are early warning signs of soft flags.

### **Chapter 4 — Creative Rotation & Pattern Breaking**

Meta detects repetitive ad patterns faster than any human reviewer.

Small, consistent variations are how you stay invisible.

### **Rotation Formula**

- Change ***one element every 3–5 days:*** image, CTA, or copy.
- Keep a library of at least ***20 creatives*** to rotate through.
- Keep Neutral ads running in the background.

### **Ad Health Tracking**

Monitor:

- ***Approval ratio (approved vs rejected)***
- ***CPM trend lines***
- ***Page feedback score***

If any of these drop sharply, rotate creatives immediately or pause risky campaigns for 24 hours.
